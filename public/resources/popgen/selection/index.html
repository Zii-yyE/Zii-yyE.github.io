Natural selection is a mechanism for generating an exceedingly high degree of improbability.

— Ronald A. Fisher

Selection is the phenomenon that individuals carrying certain phenotypes leave more offspring on average. The multiple-to-multiple map between genetic loci and phenotypes (polygenicity and pleiotropy) plus recombination complicates the signature of selection in the genome.
